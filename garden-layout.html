Planting Key — How to Plant Each Square Foot

Each grid cell above is approximately 1 sq ft. Below shows how to arrange seeds or plants within each square. Dots represent individual seeds/plants. Spacing is based on your seed packet recommendations.

Tomato (2 sq ft)
1 plant per 2 sq ft
Center in the 2 ft space. Install cage or stake at planting. Prune suckers for indeterminate types. Mulch around base.
Cucumber (trellised)
1 plant per sq ft
Plant 12" apart along trellis base. Train vines up trellis as they grow. Pinch laterals below 2 ft.
Pole Bean (trellised)
2–3 plants per sq ft
Sow seeds 4–6" apart in a row along the trellis base, 1" deep. Thin to strongest. Train up trellis or poles.
Sugar Snap Pea (trellised)
8 plants per sq ft
Sow in 2 rows, 2–3" apart, 1" deep. Double row along trellis maximizes yield in the 4 ft width.
Hot Pepper
1 plant per sq ft
Center one transplant per square. Stake if needed once fruiting. Peppers like warm soil — south edge is ideal.
Onion
9 plants per sq ft
Plant transplants in a 3×3 grid, ~4" apart. Push sets 1" deep. Patterson and Walla Walla can share rows.
Carrot
16 seeds per sq ft
Sow in a 4×4 grid, ¼" deep. Thin to 2–3" apart once tops are 2" tall. Keep soil loose and stone-free for straight roots.
Radish
16 seeds per sq ft
Sow ½" deep in a 4×4 grid. No thinning needed for Pepito. Harvest in 3–4 weeks, then resow immediately for succession.
Lettuce
4 heads per sq ft
Space 6" apart in a 2×2 pattern. Surface sow (needs light), press gently. Mix varieties across adjacent squares.
Spinach
9 plants per sq ft
Sow ½" deep in 3×3 grid. For baby spinach, pack tighter (16/sq ft). Succession sow every 2–3 weeks.
Kale
1 plant per sq ft
Center one plant. Winter Wonderland gets big — harvest outer leaves regularly to keep compact. Very productive per plant. Only 1 plant needed (2 sq ft).
Potato
1 seed potato per sq ft
Plant 4" deep, eyes up. Hill 4–6" of soil/straw as stems emerge (2–3 times). Each plant needs 12" space.
Garlic (in ground)
4–6 cloves per sq ft
Already planted. Rows are spaced ~6" apart — wide enough to interplant lettuce between rows in spring.
Garlic + Lettuce
3 lettuce between garlic rows
Sow lettuce seeds between existing garlic rows in March. Harvest lettuce May–June before garlic matures. Garlic repels aphids.
Broccoli (fall crop)
1 plant per 1.5–2 sq ft
Transplant mid-July into post-pea zone. Give 18–24" between plants. Harvest main head, then side shoots for weeks.
Dill
2–3 plants per sq ft
Sow ¼" deep. Direct sow below cucumbers — ready for pickling at the same time. Succession sow every 2–3 weeks.
Cilantro
~6 plants per sq ft
Sow ¼–½" deep. Calypso is slow-bolt but still succession sow every 2–3 weeks. Let some go to seed for coriander.
Basil
1 plant per sq ft
Center one nursery transplant. Pinch flowers to encourage bushy growth. Place directly next to tomatoes.
Marigold
1 plant per sq ft
Buy starts locally. Place along south edge near tomatoes and peppers. Deadhead for continuous blooming.
Nasturtium
1 plant per sq ft
Sow ½–1" deep. Cover seeds — needs darkness to germinate. Mounding type fills the square. Edible flowers!
Zinnia
1 plant per sq ft
Sow ¼" deep after last frost. Benary's Giant gets tall (2–3 ft) — place at bed ends so they don't shade crops. Cut flowers to encourage more blooms.
Rhubarb (4 sq ft)
1 crown per 4 sq ft (2×2 block)
Already established at southwest corner of Plot A. Spreads 3–4 ft wide. Pull (don't cut) stalks. Remove flower stalks promptly.
Parsley
1–2 plants per sq ft
Start indoors early (slow germination — 2–4 weeks). Or direct sow ¼" deep. Giant of Italy is very productive.
Sage
1 plant per sq ft
Buy nursery transplant. Perennial — will come back each year. Harvest leaves as needed. Prefers well-drained soil.

Layout Rationale & Companion Planting Notes

Plot A — Warm Crops (4 ft × 19.5 ft, long side north)

  • 19.5 ft trellis along north edge: Excellent trellis run — 8 ft of pole beans on the west end, 10 ft of cucumbers (5 pickling + 5 slicing) on the east. Nasturtiums bookend the trellis as trap crops.
  • EAST ZONE — Tomatoes + Basil + Peppers + Marigolds: The star companion cluster occupies the east ~6 ft. Basil planted directly adjacent to tomatoes. Hot peppers share the warm south edge. Marigolds border the tomato/pepper area to repel aphids and nematodes.
  • CENTER ZONE — Dill below cucumbers / Carrots + Onions: Dill (3 sq ft) is planted directly south of the cucumber trellis — ready for pickling at the same time. Carrots and onions share the center-south area for mutual pest protection (onion fly / carrot fly).
  • WEST ZONE — Rhubarb + Herbs + Radishes: Rhubarb occupies its established 2×2 ft corner at the southwest. Sage, cilantro, and parsley cluster nearby. Radishes fill in for fast succession harvesting.
  • Narrower bed (4 ft): With only 4 rows of depth, every crop is within arm's reach from either side — no stepping into the bed needed.

Plot B — Cool Crops (4 ft × 19.5 ft, short side north, runs N–S, slopes uphill to south)

  • ⛰️ Slope & moisture strategy: The south end is higher than the north, so water drains downhill toward the north (low) end. Moisture-loving crops (peas, spinach, lettuce) are intentionally placed at the low/moist north end. Drought-tolerant crops (garlic, kale) sit at the drier high/south end. Potatoes bridge the middle — they want even moisture early but benefit from drying out as they mature. Tip: water the south/high end more frequently or add extra mulch there to retain moisture.
  • Pea trellis on 4 ft north edge (low end) → fall broccoli: ~16 pea plants along the short north face through spring. Peas love consistent moisture — the low end is ideal. When peas finish (~late June), pull vines, remove trellis, work in compost, and transplant 3–4 broccoli starts in mid-July. Peas fix nitrogen in the soil, and broccoli is a heavy nitrogen feeder — ideal succession pair. The low/moist end also suits broccoli's need for consistent moisture. Harvest broccoli main heads Sep–Oct, then side shoots through November.
  • Spinach adjacent to peas (north/low): Benefits from pea shade as weather warms, and from the extra moisture at the low end. Both cool-season crops planted early together.
  • Lettuce in the center (~5 ft zone): Large succession-planting zone running through the middle of the bed. Gets moderate moisture from the slope. Rotate through all 3 varieties every 2–3 weeks.
  • Potatoes in the center-south (expanded): Canada Gold (9 cells), Carla Rose (8 cells), and Nicola (2 cells) — 19 potato plants total. Separated from tomatoes in Plot A (same family — shared disease risk). Moderate drainage here helps prevent rot. Stop watering when foliage yellows. Nicola is a waxy yellow variety great for salads and roasting.
  • Kale + garlic (south/high, drier): Garlic (already in ground, ~5 ft at south end) sits directly adjacent to the single kale plant (Winter Wonderland, 2 sq ft in Row 1). Garlic actually prefers to dry out as it matures — the higher/drier end is ideal for it. Garlic deters cabbage moths and aphids that target kale. One kale plant with regular harvesting of outer leaves provides plenty for a family.
  • 🥬 Phase 1 — Interplant lettuce between garlic rows (March–June): Direct sow lettuce between the garlic rows now. Garlic rows are spaced wide enough for lettuce to grow between them. Lettuce stays shallow-rooted and won't compete. Garlic repels aphids that bother lettuce. Harvest lettuce by May–June, before garlic matures. Shown as split-color cells (garlic/green) in the layout.
  • 🔄 Phase 2 — After garlic harvest (mid-July → fall): Once garlic is pulled (~mid-July), the allium residue left in the soil naturally deters aphids and cabbage pests. Replant with: (1) Fall lettuce succession — sow every 2–3 weeks from mid-July through August for continuous harvest into October; (2) Fall spinach — sow late August; (3) Quick radishes — sow late August for ~3 week harvest. This gives you an extra 5 ft of productive greens space for the second half of the season.
  • Nasturtiums flanking potatoes & kale: Placed adjacent to the potato/kale zone to draw aphids and cabbage moths away from brassicas and deter potato beetles. Nasturtiums tolerate dry conditions well.
  • N–S orientation advantage: Morning and afternoon sun hits both long sides. Taller plants (peas) at the north end won't shade the rest of the bed.

Succession Planting Reminders

  • Lettuce: New sowing every 2–3 weeks, March through September (shade in summer).
  • Spinach: Every 2–3 weeks in spring; pause midsummer; resume late August.
  • Radishes: Every 10–14 days, spring and fall.
  • Cilantro: Every 2–3 weeks to stay ahead of bolting.
  • Dill: Every 2–3 weeks starting after last frost, for continuous supply through pickling season.
  • Pole beans: Second sowing 2–3 weeks after first for extended harvest.
  • Carrots: Every 3 weeks for continuous harvest.
