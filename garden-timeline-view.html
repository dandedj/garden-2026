Garden Timeline 2026

Issaquah, WA · Zone 8b · Two raised beds (4 × 19.5 ft) · Last frost ~Apr 15 · First frost ~Oct 15

Late February – Early March Soil: 40–45°F NOW
Garden Prep
  • ✓Clean out garden beds — Removed debris and old plant material from both plots.
  • ✓Amend soil with compost — Spread 2–3″ compost over both beds, worked into top 6–8″. Extra on Plot B south end.
  • Test soil pH — Target 6.0–7.0. Add lime if acidic.
  • Mulch Plot B south end — 2–3″ straw mulch around garlic once planted.
  • Order/gather supplies — Seed trays, potting mix, row cover, stakes, tomato cages.
Start Indoors
  • Parsley — Giant of Italy — Slow germinator (14–28 days). Soil 65–75°F.
Early–Mid March (Mar 1–15) Soil: 42–48°F
Cool-Season Direct Sow
  • Peas — Sugar Snap — 1″ deep, 2–3″ apart along Plot B trellis (north end). Soil 45°F+.
  • Spinach — Space — ½″ deep, 2–4″ apart, Plot B north.
  • Radishes — Pepito — ½″ deep, 1–2″ apart, Plot A west.
  • Lettuce — 3 varieties — ⅛″ deep, barely cover. Plot B center. Won’t germinate above 75°F.
  • Lettuce — between garlic rows — Plot B south end. Garlic repels aphids. Harvest by May–June.
Transplants & Sets
  • Onion plants arrive (~3/16) — Patterson & Walla Walla. 1″ deep, 4–5″ apart. Plot A south.
  • Potatoes — Canada Gold & Carla Rose — Cut 2 days early to callus. 4″ deep, 12″ apart. Soil 45°F+. Plot B east.
Infrastructure
  • Install pea trellis (Plot B) — Before peas reach 4–6″. Netting or string.
Late March (Mar 16–31) Soil: 48–52°F
Direct Sow
  • Carrots — Yaya — ¼″ deep, thin to 1–2″. Soil 55°F+. Plot A center.
  • Kale — Red Russian (1–2 plants only) — Sow 3–4 seeds, thin to best 1–2 plants, 18–24″ apart. Plot B south-center.
  • Cilantro — Calypso (1st) — ¼–½″ deep, 6–8″ apart. Plot A west-center.
Succession Sowings
  • Spinach — 2nd sowing
  • Lettuce — 2nd sowing — Stagger varieties.
  • Radishes — 2nd sowing — Every 10–14 days.
Fertilizing
  • Feed cool-season crops — 5-5-5 or fish emulsion once 2–3 true leaves.
Early April (Apr 1–15) Soil: 50–55°F
Succession Sowings
  • Carrots — 2nd sowing
  • Cilantro — 2nd sowing
  • Radishes — 3rd sowing
  • Lettuce — 3rd sowing
Start Indoors
  • Cucumbers — Gershwin & Gateway — 3–4 wks before transplant. Heat mat. 70–85°F.
  • Nasturtiums — Alaska Mix — Darkness required for germination.
  • Zinnias — Benary’s Giant — 4–6 wks before last frost. 70–80°F.
Infrastructure & Fertilizing
  • Install Plot A trellis — For pole beans and cucumbers.
  • Prepare tomato cages (6)
  • Feed peas — bone meal at flowering
  • Hill potatoes (1st hilling) — When stems 6–8″.
Mid–Late April (Apr 15–30) Soil: 55–60°F
LAST FROST ~APR 15 — Warm-Season Direct Sow
  • Dill — Bouquet (1st) — ¼″ deep, 6–12″ apart. In front of cucumber trellis.
  • Pole Beans — Fortex & Seychelles (1st) — 1″ deep, 4–6″ apart along Plot A trellis. Soil 60°F+.
  • Nasturtiums — direct sow or transplant
Succession Sowings
  • Spinach — 3rd sowing
  • Lettuce — 4th sowing
  • Radishes — 4th sowing
  • Cilantro — 3rd sowing
Early May (May 1–15) Soil: 58–63°F
Transplant Outdoors
  • Cucumbers — Gershwin & Gateway — Harden off 5–7 days. 12″ apart on trellis.
  • Parsley — Giant of Italy — 8–10″ apart, Plot A west-center.
Direct Sow & Succession
  • Zinnias, Dill 2nd, Beans 2nd, Carrots 3rd, Lettuce 5th, Cilantro 4th
Maintenance
  • Train beans & cucumbers on trellis
  • Hill potatoes (2nd hilling)
Mid–Late May (May 15–31) Soil: 60–68°F
Nursery Transplants
  • Tomatoes — Plant deep (⅔ stem), 24″ apart, Plot A east. Install cages.
  • Hot Peppers — Plot A south-east.
  • Basil — Next to tomatoes. Wait for nights above 50°F.
  • Sage — Plot A west-center.
  • Marigolds — South edge of tomato zone, 10–12″ apart.
Fertilizing
  • Feed tomatoes at planting — Bone meal + compost in hole.
  • Feed cucumbers & beans at flowering
June Soil: 62–70°F
Harvest Begins!
  • Radishes, Lettuce, Spinach, Peas, Kale, Garlic scapes
Succession & Fall Prep
  • Dill 4th, Cilantro 5th, Carrots 4th, Beans 3rd (final)
  • Start broccoli seeds indoors (mid-June)
Maintenance
  • Feed tomatoes, cucumbers, kale. Hill potatoes (final). Add shade cloth. Remove pea trellis.
July — Peak Season Soil: 65–75°F
Peak Harvest
  • Cucumbers, Beans, Dill, Carrots, Walla Walla Onions, Garlic, Potatoes (early), Tomatoes (first)
Transplant & Replanting
  • Broccoli — mid-July — 3–4 transplants in former pea zone, Plot B north.
  • Fall lettuce & radishes in former garlic zone — Plot B south.
Canning Season
  • Make pickles! — Gershwin cucumbers + Bouquet dill + garlic.
  • Freeze/can beans
August Soil: 65–72°F
Full-Swing Harvest
  • Tomatoes (peak), Hot Peppers, Patterson Onions, Potatoes (full), Rhubarb (final)
Fall Planting
  • Fall lettuce 2nd, Fall spinach, Spinach resume, Lettuce resume, Radishes resume, Carrots (final)
September Soil: 58–65°F
Late-Season Harvest
  • Tomatoes, Cucumbers (winding down), Beans (final), Carrots, Kale, Fall lettuce, Broccoli (main heads)
Seed Saving & Cleanup
  • Collect nasturtium & zinnia seeds. Remove spent warm-season crops. Compost healthy debris.
October — Season’s End Soil: 48–55°F
Final Harvests (before frost ~Oct 15)
  • Green tomatoes, Hot peppers, Carrots (mulch to overwinter), Kale (overwinters!), Broccoli side shoots
Garden Closeout
  • Remove annuals, trellises. Plant garlic for 2027. Lay fall compost. Cover crop (optional). Mulch beds.
