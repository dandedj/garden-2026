# Garden Timeline — 2026

**Issaquah, WA · USDA Zone 8b · PNW Maritime Climate**
Two raised beds, 4 ft × 19.5 ft each (Plot A: long side north; Plot B: short side north, runs N–S)
Last frost: ~April 15 · First fall frost: ~October 15 · Growing season: ~180 days

---

## Late February — Early March (Now!)

### Garden Prep
- [ ] **Test & amend soil** — Test pH (target 6.0–7.0). Add lime if acidic (common in PNW).
- [ ] **Lay compost** — Spread 2–3 inches of finished compost over both beds and work into the top 6–8 inches. Do this 2–4 weeks before first plantings so nutrients activate. On Plot B, add extra compost to the south/high end to improve water retention on the slope.
- [ ] **Mulch Plot B south end** — The south end of Plot B is higher and dries out faster. Plan to add 2–3" of straw mulch around garlic and kale once planted to help retain moisture on the uphill side.
- [ ] **Order/gather supplies** — Seed starting trays, potting mix, row cover fabric, stakes, tomato cages.
- [ ] **Start parsley indoors** — Sow Giant of Italy parsley indoors under lights. Slow germinator (14–28 days). Soil temp 65–75°F.

**Soil temp (late Feb):** ~40–45°F

---

## Early–Mid March (Mar 1–15)

### Planting — Cool-Season Direct Sow
- [ ] **Peas — Sugar Snap** — Direct sow as soon as soil is workable. Soil temp needed: 45°F+. Projected soil temp: ~45°F by early March. Sow 1" deep, 2–3" apart along Plot B trellis (north end).
- [ ] **Spinach — Space** — Direct sow. Soil temp needed: 45°F+. Sow ½" deep, 2–4" apart, Plot B north end.
- [ ] **Radishes — Pepito** — Direct sow. Soil temp needed: 40°F+. Sow ½" deep, 1–2" apart, Plot A west side.
- [ ] **Lettuce — all 3 varieties** — Direct sow or start indoors. Soil temp needed: 40°F+ (won't germinate above 75°F). Sow ⅛" deep, barely cover. Plot B center.
- [ ] **Lettuce — interplant between garlic rows (Plot B south end)** — Direct sow lettuce between the garlic rows now. Garlic is spaced wide enough for lettuce to grow between. Garlic repels aphids — a natural companion. Harvest this lettuce by May–June before garlic matures.

### Planting — Transplants & Sets
- [ ] **Onion plants arrive** — Patterson & Walla Walla ship week of 3/16. Plant 1" deep, 4–5" apart as soon as they arrive. Plot A south row.
- [ ] **Potatoes — Canada Gold & Carla Rose** — Cut seed potatoes 2 days before planting to callus. Plant 4" deep, 12" apart when soil hits 45°F+. Plot B east side.

### Infrastructure
- [ ] **Install Plot B pea trellis** — Set up before peas reach 4–6 inches tall. Peas climb with tendrils; provide netting or string.

**Soil temp (early March):** ~42–48°F

---

## Late March (Mar 16–31)

### Planting — Cool Season Continued
- [ ] **Carrots — Yaya** — Direct sow. Soil temp needed: 55°F+ (may need to wait for soil to warm a bit more, or use row cover). Sow ¼" deep, thin to 1–2" apart. Plot A center.
- [ ] **Kale — Red Russian** — Direct sow or start indoors. Soil temp needed: 55°F+. Sow ¼–½" deep, thin to 12–18" apart. Plot B south-center.
- [ ] **Cilantro — Calypso (1st sowing)** — Direct sow. Soil temp needed: 55°F+. Sow ¼–½" deep, 6–8" apart. Plot A west-center.

### Succession Planting
- [ ] **Spinach — 2nd sowing** (if 1st sowing is 2–3 weeks old)
- [ ] **Lettuce — 2nd sowing** (stagger varieties)
- [ ] **Radishes — 2nd sowing** (every 10–14 days)

### Garden Prep
- [ ] **Fertilize cool-season crops** — Side-dress spinach, lettuce, kale, and peas with balanced organic fertilizer (e.g., 5-5-5 or fish emulsion) once seedlings have 2–3 true leaves.

**Soil temp (late March):** ~48–52°F

---

## Early April (Apr 1–15)

### Planting — Transition Period
- [ ] **Carrots — 2nd succession sowing** (every 3 weeks)
- [ ] **Cilantro — 2nd sowing** (every 2–3 weeks to stay ahead of bolting)
- [ ] **Radishes — 3rd sowing**
- [ ] **Lettuce — 3rd sowing**

### Indoor Starts (for later transplant)
- [ ] **Start cucumbers indoors** — Sow Gershwin & Gateway 3–4 weeks before transplant date (~May 1). Soil temp needed: 70–85°F. Use heat mat.
- [ ] **Start dill indoors** (optional) — Or wait to direct sow after last frost. Soil temp needed: 60–70°F.
- [ ] **Start nasturtiums indoors** — Sow ½–1" deep in pots 2–3 weeks before last frost. Darkness required for germination; cover pots.
- [ ] **Start zinnias indoors** — Sow ¼" deep 4–6 weeks before last frost. Soil temp needed: 70–80°F.

### Infrastructure
- [ ] **Install Plot A trellis** — Set up the full-length north-edge trellis for pole beans and cucumbers. Get it in place before transplanting.
- [ ] **Prepare tomato cages** — Have 6 sturdy cages (at least 5 ft tall) ready for mid-May planting.

### Fertilizing
- [ ] **Feed peas** — Peas fix their own nitrogen but benefit from a light phosphorus feed (bone meal) at flowering.
- [ ] **Hill potatoes (first hilling)** — When potato stems are 6–8" tall, mound soil around them leaving 3–4" of foliage exposed.

**Soil temp (early April):** ~50–55°F

---

## Mid–Late April (Apr 15–30) — LAST FROST ~Apr 15

### Planting — After Last Frost
- [ ] **Dill — Bouquet (1st sowing)** — Direct sow after last frost. Soil temp needed: 60°F+. Sow ¼" deep, 6–12" apart directly in front of cucumber trellis area (Plot A center-north).
- [ ] **Pole Beans — Fortex & Seychelles (1st sowing)** — Direct sow when soil hits 60°F+. Sow 1" deep, 4–6" apart along Plot A trellis (west end). May need to wait until late April if soil is still cool.
- [ ] **Nasturtiums — Alaska Mix** — Direct sow ½–1" deep, or transplant indoor starts. Soil temp needed: 55–65°F. Place near beans (Plot A) and flanking kale (Plot B).

### Succession Planting
- [ ] **Spinach — 3rd sowing**
- [ ] **Lettuce — 4th sowing**
- [ ] **Radishes — 4th sowing**
- [ ] **Cilantro — 3rd sowing**

### Fertilizing
- [ ] **Side-dress leafy greens** — Apply fish emulsion or liquid kelp to lettuce, spinach, and kale every 3–4 weeks through the season.

**Soil temp (late April):** ~55–60°F

---

## Early May (May 1–15)

### Planting — Warm Season Transplants
- [ ] **Cucumbers — transplant outdoors** — Harden off indoor starts for 5–7 days. Transplant when soil is 60°F+ and night temps stay above 50°F. Space 12" apart on Plot A trellis.
- [ ] **Parsley — transplant outdoors** — Move indoor-started parsley to Plot A west-center. Space 8–10" apart.

### Planting — Direct Sow
- [ ] **Zinnias — Benary's Giant Mix** — Direct sow or transplant starts. Soil temp needed: 70°F+. May need to wait until mid-May in cooler years. Sow ¼" deep, 12–18" apart at east ends of both beds.
- [ ] **Dill — 2nd sowing** (succession every 2–3 weeks)

### Succession Planting
- [ ] **Pole Beans — 2nd sowing** (2–3 weeks after first, for extended harvest)
- [ ] **Carrots — 3rd sowing**
- [ ] **Lettuce — 5th sowing** (consider shade cloth as temps rise)
- [ ] **Cilantro — 4th sowing**

### Infrastructure & Maintenance
- [ ] **Train beans & cucumbers on trellis** — Gently guide vines as they reach for support.
- [ ] **Hill potatoes (second hilling)** — Add more soil when stems grow another 6–8".

**Soil temp (early May):** ~58–63°F

---

## Mid–Late May (May 15–31)

### Planting — Warm Season Transplants (from nursery)
- [ ] **Tomatoes — transplant** — Buy starts from local nursery. Soil temp needed: 60°F+, night temps consistently above 50°F. Plant deep (bury ⅔ of stem) in Plot A east zone, 24" apart. Install cages immediately.
- [ ] **Hot peppers — transplant** — Buy starts from nursery. Same warm-soil requirements as tomatoes. Plant in Plot A south-east edge.
- [ ] **Basil — transplant** — Buy starts from nursery. Basil is very frost-tender; wait until night temps are reliably above 50°F. Plant directly adjacent to tomatoes (Plot A east, col 15).
- [ ] **Sage — transplant** — Buy start from nursery. Plant in Plot A west-center.
- [ ] **Marigolds — transplant** — Buy French marigold starts from nursery. Plant 10–12" apart along south edge of tomato/pepper zone (Plot A east).

### Succession Planting
- [ ] **Dill — 3rd sowing**
- [ ] **Radishes — 5th sowing** (last spring round; resume in late August)
- [ ] **Lettuce — 6th sowing** (use shade cloth)

### Fertilizing
- [ ] **Feed tomatoes at planting** — Add a handful of bone meal and compost to each planting hole. Do not over-fertilize with nitrogen (promotes leaves over fruit).
- [ ] **Feed cucumbers** — Side-dress with compost or balanced fertilizer when vines begin to run.
- [ ] **Side-dress beans** — Light phosphorus feed (bone meal) once flowering starts.

**Soil temp (late May):** ~60–68°F

---

## June

### Maintenance & Succession
- [ ] **Dill — 4th sowing** (early June)
- [ ] **Cilantro — 5th sowing** (early June; cilantro bolts quickly in warm weather)
- [ ] **Carrots — 4th sowing** (early June)
- [ ] **Lettuce — pause or use bolt-resistant varieties** under shade cloth
- [ ] **Spinach — pause plantings** (too warm; resume late August)
- [ ] **Beans — 3rd sowing** (early June for final succession)
- [ ] **Start broccoli seeds indoors (mid-June)** — Or plan to buy 3–4 transplants from the nursery in early July. Good PNW varieties: Belstar, Green Magic, Waltham 29.

### Harvest Begins!
- [ ] **Radishes** — First spring sowings ready (~21–30 days from sowing). Harvest when roots are marble-sized.
  - *Estimated: late March / early April sowings → harvest by late April–May*
- [ ] **Lettuce** — First heads ready ~55 days from sowing.
  - *Estimated: March 1 sowing → harvest by late April; continue with succession harvests*
- [ ] **Spinach** — Ready ~40–45 days from sowing.
  - *Estimated: March 1 sowing → harvest by mid-April; continue picking outer leaves*
- [ ] **Peas — Sugar Snap** — Ready ~58–70 days from sowing.
  - *Estimated: March 1 sowing → harvest late May through June*
- [ ] **Kale — Red Russian** — Baby leaves at 50 days, full at 60.
  - *Estimated: Late March sowing → baby kale harvest mid-May; continue all season*
- [ ] **Garlic scapes** — Snip curly scapes when they appear (mid-June) to redirect energy to bulbs. Use in cooking!

### Fertilizing
- [ ] **Feed tomatoes (first)** — When first fruits are golf-ball sized, side-dress with calcium-rich fertilizer (e.g., tomato-tone or bone meal). Repeat every 3–4 weeks.
- [ ] **Feed cucumbers** — Repeat side-dressing every 3–4 weeks once fruiting.
- [ ] **Feed kale & greens** — Fish emulsion or liquid kelp every 3–4 weeks.
- [ ] **Hill potatoes (final hilling)** — Last mounding. Stop hilling once plants flower.

### Infrastructure
- [ ] **Remove pea trellis & prep for broccoli** — Sugar snap peas wind down by late June. Remove spent vines and trellis. Work 1–2" of compost into the former pea zone. The nitrogen peas fixed in the soil is perfect for heavy-feeding broccoli.
- [ ] **Add shade cloth** — Protect lettuce from bolting in the summer heat. Drape 30–50% shade cloth over lettuce section.

---

## July

### Harvest — Peak Season
- [ ] **Cucumbers — Gershwin (pickling)** — ~52 days from transplant.
  - *Estimated: May 1 transplant → first harvest late June; peak July*
  - Pick at 2–4" for pickles; harvest every 1–2 days to keep production going.
- [ ] **Cucumbers — Gateway (slicing)** — ~58 days from transplant.
  - *Estimated: May 1 transplant → first harvest early July*
  - Pick at 8–10" for best flavor.
- [ ] **Pole Beans — Fortex & Seychelles** — ~55–60 days from sowing.
  - *Estimated: late April sowing → first harvest late June; peak July*
  - Pick pods at 7–10" (Fortex stays tender even at 11"). Harvest every 2–3 days.
- [ ] **Dill** — ~45–55 days from sowing. Harvest leaves anytime; flower heads for pickling.
  - *Ready for pickling season right alongside cucumbers*
- [ ] **Cilantro** — Harvest leaves at ~50 days. Let some bolt for coriander seeds.
- [ ] **Carrots — Yaya** — ~55–60 days from sowing.
  - *Estimated: late March sowing → first harvest late May/June; succession harvests July–Oct*
- [ ] **Potatoes — early check** — ~70–90 days from planting.
  - *Estimated: mid-March planting → new potatoes (small) by late May/June; full harvest July–August*
  - Dig "new potatoes" anytime after flowering. For full harvest, wait until foliage yellows and dies back.
- [ ] **Onions — Walla Walla** — ~90–100 days from transplant.
  - *Estimated: late March transplant → harvest late June/July*
  - Harvest when tops flop over. Cure in shade for 1–2 weeks.
- [ ] **Garlic — harvest** — Dig when lower 3–4 leaves are brown but upper leaves still green (~mid-July). Cure 2–4 weeks in a dry, shaded spot.
  - *Pull the interplanted lettuce first if any remains.*
- [ ] **Tomatoes (first ripe)** — ~65–85 days from transplant depending on variety.
  - *Estimated: mid-May transplant → first ripe tomatoes mid-July to early August*

### Broccoli Transplant (Plot B north end, former pea zone)
- [ ] **Transplant broccoli — mid-July** — Plant 3–4 transplants at 18–24" apart in the former pea zone (Plot B north end). The nitrogen-rich soil from peas is ideal. The low/moist north end provides the consistent moisture broccoli needs. Water deeply at transplant. ~60–80 days to main head harvest → late Sep–Oct. Side shoots continue for weeks after.
- [ ] **Fertilize broccoli at transplant** — Side-dress with balanced fertilizer or compost. Broccoli is a heavy feeder.

### Post-Garlic Replanting (Plot B south end, ~5 ft)
- [ ] **After garlic harvest (~mid-July): amend and replant** — Work 1" of compost into the former garlic zone. The allium residue in the soil naturally deters aphids and cabbage pests — ideal for fall greens.
- [ ] **Fall lettuce — 1st sowing** — Direct sow in former garlic zone. ~55 days to harvest → ready by mid-September.
- [ ] **Fall radishes** — Tuck a quick sowing alongside the lettuce. Ready in ~3 weeks (early August).

### Canning & Pickling Season
- [ ] **Make pickles!** — Gershwin cucumbers + Bouquet dill + garlic. Peak pickling window: July.
- [ ] **Freeze/can beans** — Blanch and freeze excess pole beans.

### Succession & Maintenance
- [ ] **Dill — 5th sowing** (early July — last sowing for pickling season)
- [ ] **Carrots — 5th sowing** (early July for fall harvest)
- [ ] **Cilantro — 6th sowing** (if desired; bolts fast in heat)
- [ ] **Prune tomato suckers** — Remove suckers below first fruit cluster for better airflow and larger tomatoes.

### Fertilizing
- [ ] **Feed tomatoes** — Side-dress with tomato fertilizer every 3–4 weeks through August.
- [ ] **Feed cucumbers** — Continue every 3–4 weeks while producing.
- [ ] **Feed beans** — Light side-dress if production slows.

---

## August

### Harvest — Full Swing
- [ ] **Tomatoes — peak harvest** — All 6 plants should be producing heavily by now.
  - Harvest regularly to encourage continued production.
  - Start saving for sauce if you have surplus.
- [ ] **Hot peppers** — Most varieties ready 70–90 days from transplant.
  - *Estimated: mid-May transplant → harvest July–September*
- [ ] **Onions — Patterson** — ~104 days from transplant.
  - *Estimated: late March transplant → harvest mid-July/August*
  - These are storage onions — cure 2–4 weeks in a dry, shaded spot.
- [ ] **Potatoes — full harvest** — Dig all remaining potatoes once foliage dies back completely.
- [ ] **Rhubarb** — Final harvest. Stop picking by late August to let plants store energy.

### Fall Planting — Start Now!
- [ ] **Fall lettuce — 2nd sowing in former garlic zone** (late August; continues the succession from the July sowing)
- [ ] **Fall spinach — sow in former garlic zone** (late August; soil cools enough for germination; allium residue deters pests)
- [ ] **Spinach — resume sowing in main Plot B area** (late August)
- [ ] **Lettuce — resume succession sowing** (late August into September)
- [ ] **Radishes — resume sowing** (late August; every 10–14 days)
- [ ] **Kale — late sowing** (if desired; kale sweetens after frost)
- [ ] **Carrots — final sowing** (early August for fall/winter harvest)

### Fertilizing
- [ ] **Last tomato feed** — Final side-dress early August. Stop fertilizing by mid-August to encourage ripening.
- [ ] **Feed fall crops** — Apply compost or balanced fertilizer to new fall plantings.

---

## September

### Harvest — Late Season
- [ ] **Tomatoes — continue harvesting** — Pick any remaining fruit before frost threat. Green tomatoes can ripen indoors.
- [ ] **Cucumbers — winding down** — Production slows as nights cool. Pick remaining fruit.
- [ ] **Beans — final harvest** — Collect remaining pods. Remove spent vines from trellis.
- [ ] **Carrots** — Fall succession carrots ready. Can leave in ground with mulch for winter harvest.
- [ ] **Kale** — Harvest increases as weather cools. Kale is sweeter after light frost.
- [ ] **Nasturtium seeds** — Collect seeds from nasturtiums for next year before first frost.
- [ ] **Zinnia seeds** — Let some flower heads dry on the plant for seed saving.
- [ ] **Flowers — last blooms** — Enjoy marigolds, nasturtiums, and zinnias until frost.

### Succession Harvests
- [ ] **Fall lettuce** — August sowings ready by late September.
- [ ] **Fall spinach** — August sowings ready by early October.
- [ ] **Fall radishes** — Quick 3–4 week crop.

### Maintenance
- [ ] **Remove spent warm-season crops** — Pull cucumber vines, bean vines, pepper plants as they finish.
- [ ] **Compost spent plants** — Add healthy plant material to compost pile (not diseased plants).

---

## October

### Harvest — Season's End
- [ ] **Green tomatoes** — Pick all remaining tomatoes before first frost (~Oct 15). Ripen indoors on a windowsill or in paper bags with a banana.
- [ ] **Hot peppers — final pick** — Harvest all remaining peppers before frost.
- [ ] **Carrots — continue or mulch** — Carrots can overwinter in ground under 4–6" of straw mulch in Zone 8b.
- [ ] **Kale — overwinters** — Red Russian kale tolerates frost well. Continue harvesting through winter with protection.
- [ ] **Onions (storage) — enjoy** — Patterson storage onions should keep through winter in a cool, dark place.

### Garden Closeout
- [ ] **Remove all annual crops** — Clear beds of spent tomato cages, trellises, and annual plant debris.
- [ ] **Remove trellises** — Take down Plot A and Plot B trellises. Clean and store for 2027.
- [ ] **Plant garlic for 2027** — Mid-October is ideal garlic planting time in Zone 8b. Plant cloves 2" deep, 6" apart. Mulch with straw.
- [ ] **Lay fall compost** — Spread 2–3 inches of compost over all beds.
- [ ] **Plant cover crop** (optional) — Sow crimson clover or winter rye in empty bed space to fix nitrogen and prevent erosion over winter.
- [ ] **Mulch beds** — Cover with 3–4" of straw or leaf mulch to protect soil over winter.
